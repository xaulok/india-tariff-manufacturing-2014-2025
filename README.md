### Abstract

Between 2014 and 2025, India deployed two successive industrial policy
frameworks — Make in India (2014–2019) and Atmanirbhar Bharat (2020–2025)
— that systematically reversed the tariff liberalisation of the 1991 reform
era. India's simple average MFN applied tariff rose from approximately 13.0%
in 2014–15 to 17.0% by 2022–23, while the share of tariff lines bearing duties
above 10% increased from 14.9% to over 26% of all lines. Yet manufacturing's
share of total GVA remained largely stagnant in a 15.5–18.5% band — far short
of the government's stated 25% target.

This paper presents the first quantitative end-to-end empirical assessment of
the tariff-manufacturing relationship in India across this full policy arc. Using
annual data from WTO, MOSPI, RBI, and PIB sources, three OLS regression
specifications are estimated with HAC-robust standard errors, supplemented by a
Chow structural break test at 2020 and sector-level case study evidence.

**Central finding:** The tariff coefficient reverses sign across the two policy
phases — β = −3.398 in Make in India (2014–2019) vs β = +1.095 in Atmanirbhar
Bharat (2020–2024) — consistent with the hypothesis that tariff protection
generates positive manufacturing output effects only when paired with
performance-linked supply-side incentives (PLI scheme).

**Keywords:** Import tariffs · Manufacturing GVA · Make in India ·
Atmanirbhar Bharat · Production Linked Incentive · Industrial policy ·
Structural break · OLS regression · India

### Key Results

| Model | Tariff Coefficient | p-value | Adj. R² | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1A: Baseline OLS | β = +0.484 | 0.249 | 0.568 | Insignificant — tariffs alone don't shift aggregate GVA share |
| 1B: Interaction (Tariff × PLI) | β = −0.0000938 | 0.368 | 0.580 | Negative sign — input cost offsetting at high tariff levels |
| 1C: Lagged Tariff | β = +0.757 | 0.092 | 0.777 | Marginally significant — 1-year investment transmission lag |
| Sub-period: MiI (2014–19) | β = −3.398 | — | — | Protection without PLI → declining manufacturing share |
| Sub-period: AB (2020–24) | β = +1.095 | — | — | Protection with PLI → rising manufacturing share |

**Chow Test at 2020:** F = 5.926, p = 0.302 (inconclusive due to n=11; Type II error likely)

### Dataset Overview

**File:** `data/india_manufacturing_dataset.csv`  
**Coverage:** India, FY 2014–15 to FY 2024–25 (annual, n = 11)

| Variable | Description | Unit | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| `year` | Financial year start (e.g., 2014 = FY2014–15) | — | — |
| `mfn_tariff` | Simple average MFN applied tariff (all goods) | % | World Bank WITS |
| `mfg_gva_share` | Manufacturing GVA as % of total GVA (const. 2011–12 prices) | % | MOSPI NAS 2025 |
| `gdp_growth` | Real GDP growth rate | % | MOSPI / RBI DBIE |
| `inr_usd` | Annual average INR per USD exchange rate | ₹ | RBI Reference Rate |
| `covid` | COVID-19 dummy (= 1 for FY2020–21 only) | 0/1 | Author |
| `pli_disbursement` | Annual PLI incentive disbursement (all 14 sectors) | ₹ crore | PIB press releases |
| `fdi_mfg` | FDI equity inflows to manufacturing | USD million | DPIIT / RBI DBIE |

**Author-constructed variables** (generated inside the R script):

| Variable | Construction |
|---|---|
| `tariff_pli` | `mfn_tariff × pli_disbursement` — interaction term |
| `mfn_lag` | One-year lag of `mfn_tariff` |
| `ab_era` | Binary: 1 for year ≥ 2020 (Atmanirbhar Bharat phase) |

> **Note on PLI disbursement:** Values for 2021–2024 are author-constructed
> annual flows derived from first differences of cumulative PIB figures. Treat
> as approximations until DPIIT publishes a formal annual series.

### How to Replicate

#### Step 1 — Clone the repository

```bash
git clone https://github.com/xaulok/india-tariff-manufacturing-2014-2025
cd india-tariff-manufacturing-2014-2025
```

#### Step 2 — Open in RStudio

```r
# Set working directory to the cloned folder
setwd("path/to/india-tariff-manufacturing-2014-2025")
```

#### Step 3 — Install required packages (run once)

```r
install.packages(c(
  "sandwich",    # HAC robust standard errors
  "lmtest",      # coeftest() for HAC-corrected output
  "stargazer",   # formatted regression tables
  "strucchange", # Chow test and CUSUM stability
  "car",         # VIF multicollinearity diagnostics
  "urca",        # Zivot-Andrews unit root test
  "ggplot2"      # publication-ready figures
))
```

#### Step 4 — Run the full analysis

```r
source("code/india_tariff_analysis.R")
```

This single command:
- Loads and validates the dataset
- Runs Models 1A, 1B, and 1C with HAC standard errors
- Performs the Chow structural break test at 2020
- Runs the Zivot-Andrews endogenous break detection test
- Performs Shapiro-Wilk, Durbin-Watson, and VIF diagnostics
- Generates all 4 figures and saves as 300 DPI PNG files
- Prints the full results summary table to console

**Expected runtime:** < 30 seconds on any modern machine.

### Limitations

1. **Small sample (n = 11):** Annual data over 11 years leaves very few
   residual degrees of freedom. Most coefficients cannot reach conventional
   significance thresholds regardless of true effect size. Results should be
   read directionally rather than inferentially.

2. **PLI disbursement measurement:** Annual PLI flows are reconstructed from
   cumulative PIB data and may contain timing errors of ±1 year.

3. **Endogeneity:** Tariff rates are policy choices potentially correlated with
   manufacturing performance (reverse causality). OLS estimates may be biased.
   An IV approach using external instruments is left for future work.

4. **No welfare estimates:** Consumer welfare costs of tariff escalation are
   not quantified. A CGE model would be required.
